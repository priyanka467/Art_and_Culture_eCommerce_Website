About Us

Women from the Mithila region of North Bihar have been making Sikki craft since the early centuries, including Sikki grass weaving. The craft is thought to have originated during the Vedic period. The grass is golden in color and grows in wet, marshy areas near rivers and ponds in the region. The grass is harvested, split, and dried in the sun for 20â€“25 days, and then colored with magenta, blue, yellow, or green. The stems are used to make artifacts, while the roots are used to extract oil for perfumery and medicine

Our Story

Artisan Works was born out of a love for art and craftsmanship. It all started with a small group of artisans who shared a vision of creating a platform where their work could be appreciated and enjoyed by people everywhere. Today, we continue to uphold that vision, connecting talented artisans with art lovers around the globe.
